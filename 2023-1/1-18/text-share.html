What is NASA?

NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration) is an independent agency of the U.S. federal government responsible for the nation's civilian space program and for aeronautics and space research. NASA was established in 1958 by President Dwight D. Eisenhower.

NASA conducts a wide range of activities, including launching satellites and probes to study the earth, the solar system, and the universe and conducting research in fields such as Earth science, planetary science, and astrobiology.

Since then, NASA has played a leading role in many of the most important achievements in space exploration, including the Apollo program that landed the first humans on the monn in 1969.
